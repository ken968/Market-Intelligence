1. PRIMARY MACROECONOMIC VECTORS
The system frames asset price movements not as isolated random walks, but as derivatives of global liquidity and cost of capital.
- YieldCurve_10Y2Y: Evaluated as $Yield_{10Y} - Yield_{2Y}$. An inversion ($<0$) is a highly reliable leading indicator of liquidity contraction and impending recessionary regimes.
- Breakeven_5Y5Y: The 5-Year, 5-Year Forward Inflation Expectation Rate. It measures market-priced inflation expectations, functioning as a proxy for the Federal Reserve's long-term credibility.
- M2_YoY & M2_MoM: The first and second derivatives of the M2 Money Supply. Asset prices (particularly Bitcoin and Gold) are treated mathematically as monetary inflation hedges; thus, M2 expansion vectors heavily weight the XGBoost decision trees.

2. ASYMMETRIC INSTITUTIONAL SENTIMENT (THE COT INDEX)
Raw Commitment of Traders (COT) net positioning is unscaled and subject to multi-year systemic growth drift. To extract true stationary "Alpha", the system computes a Rolling Min-Max Scaled COT Index over a 756-trading-day (approx. 3 years) window:
$$ \text{COT\_Index}_t = \frac{\text{Net\_Commercial}_t - \min(\text{Net\_Commercial}_{t-756:t})}{\max(\text{Net\_Commercial}_{t-756:t}) - \min(\text{Net\_Commercial}_{t-756:t}) + \epsilon} \times 100 $$
This transformation isolates cyclic extremes, revealing whether institutional "Smart Money" is currently at maximum historical accumulation or maximum historical distribution, bounded strictly between 0 and 100.

3. SYNTHETIC DIVERGENCE: THE SMART MONEY SENTIMENT GAP
The crown jewel of the system's quantitative feature engineering is the `Smart_Money_Sentiment_Gap`. It is mathematically defined as:
$$ \Delta S_t = \text{Fear\_Greed\_Index}_t - \text{COT\_Index}_t $$

- A highly positive $\Delta S_t$ (e.g., +80) indicates extreme Retail Euphoria juxtaposed against extreme Institutional Shorting (Hedging/Distribution). This is a severe bearish divergence.

4. ADVANCED VOLATILITY ESTIMATION (GARMAN-KLASS)
Standard historical volatility relies solely on Close-to-Close returns, ignoring intraday variance. The system implements the Garman-Klass Volatility estimator, providing a much more robust measure of true market uncertainty:
$$ \sigma_{GK}^2 = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \ln\left(\frac{H}{L}\right) \right]^2 - (2 \ln 2 - 1) \left[ \ln\left(\frac{C}{O}\right) \right]^2 $$
Where H, L, C, and O represent High, Low, Close, and Open prices. This serves as a critical contextual feature for the Meta-Learner to adjust the magnitude of predicted returns.
